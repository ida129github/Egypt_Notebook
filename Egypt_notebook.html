Jebel Silsila

Coordinates: 24.633°N, 32.933°E

  • Narrowest part of the Nile Valley; historically dangerous for navigation.
  • Site of ancient cults for water deities; shrines and stelae line both banks.
  • Remarkable ancient quarries with visible chisel marks and eyeholes for mooring boats.
  • Supplied stone for major temples including Karnak, Luxor, Kom Ombo, and Edfu.
  • Still largely unknown to tourists despite modern infrastructure.

Temple of Philae (Aswan)

Coordinates: 24.02083°N, 32.88944°E

  • Temple dedicated to the goddess Isis, relocated to Agilkia Island.
  • Ptolemaic and Roman architecture with detailed reliefs.
  • One of the last centers of ancient Egyptian worship, active until the 6th century AD.
  • Part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site from Abu Simbel to Philae.

Rock Carvings at El-Hosh

Coordinates: Approx. 24.5°N, 32.9°E (near Edfu)

  • Prehistoric rock art site dating from 16,000 to 7,000 years ago.
  • Includes fish trap motifs, geometric designs, and naturalistic animal engravings.
  • Represents one of the oldest known rock art sites in North Africa.
  • Extensively studied by archaeologists since the 1930s.

Kom Ombo Temple

Coordinates: 24.4512°N, 32.9235°E

  • Temple dedicated to Sobek (crocodile god) and Horus (falcon-headed god).
  • Unique double temple structure with symmetrical design.
  • Famous for reliefs depicting ancient surgical instruments and medical practices.
  • Located directly on the Nile, offering scenic river views.

Edfu - Temple of Horus

Coordinates: 25.2922° N, 32.5549° E

  • One of the best-preserved temples in Egypt, built during the Ptolemaic period (237–57 BC).
  • Dedicated to Horus, the falcon-headed god of protection and kingship.
  • Features a massive entrance pylon, hypostyle halls, open court, and granite sanctum.
  • Central site for the “Feast of the Joyous Union” between Horus and Hathor.
  • Famous for detailed inscriptions and scenes depicting divine rituals and mythology.
  • Calèche (horse-drawn carriage) tours offer charming access through Edfu town.

Esna - Temple of Khnum & Barrage

Coordinates: 25.2933° N, 32.5492° E

  • Temple of Khnum built during the Ptolemaic and Roman periods; only the vestibule remains.
  • Features 24 elaborate columns with unique capitals and richly decorated with Roman reliefs.
  • Painted ceiling includes vivid zodiac signs and winged deities uncovered during restoration.
  • Inscriptions of Emperor Hadrian and others document Roman patronage of the temple.
  • The Old Esna Barrage (built in 1908) is still active and offers an exciting lock passage experience for Nile cruise travelers.

Deities of Ancient Egyptian Civilization

  • Ra: Sun god.
  • Osiris: God of the underworld.
  • Isis: Goddess of motherhood and protection.
  • Horus: Sky god, protector of pharaohs.
  • Anubis: God of embalming and funerals.
  • Thoth: God of writing and wisdom.
  • Hathor: Goddess of love and music.
  • Bastet: Goddess of home and fertility, protector in cat form.
